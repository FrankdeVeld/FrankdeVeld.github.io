Frank de Veld

I am Frank de Veld, currently a third year PhD student at Université Côte d'Azur, but working mostly with INRIA and Thales Alenia Space. I am mostly specialised in satellite trajectory optimisation, with research topics including low-thrust collision avoidance, debris removal with solar sailing, and interplanetary transfers. My studies also covered a variety of other science and engineering topics, such as planetary sciences, space systems engineering, numerical modelling, quantum physics and dynamical systems. I like to perform independent and collaborative research on space-related topics. I have been Lead Rover Engineer for TU Delft student team Lunar Zebro for 1 year, worked as a research intern in the geology research lab of Queen's University, and launched the space sustainability start-up Ecosmic, which together with my academic background have prepared me for professional life in the space industry. Apart from these rather technical topics, I like to write blog articles about topics like space sustainability and space exploration, but also physics: I wrote about space tourism, entrepeneurship and lunar rovers, to give an idea. Currently, I mostly work in Julia and Python for large projects and day-to-day tasks, but I have experience in C++, R, MatLab and Java as well and an open for new learning opportunities. If you are interested in collaboration or know of any interesting opportunities, feel free to reach out!
